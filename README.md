# BEMA-analysis
BEMA (Brief Electricity and Magnetism
Assesment) is used currently in PHYS 133 as an
assessment tool at the end of the course.from this
assessment, the instructors can see what the
students would know and what they do not. What
we are interested in this paper is what type of
knowledge described by Shavelson and others is or
is not being activated on each item (further
described in the Factor Analysis section. This is
done by first satisfying the condition that the
assessment is a "good" assessment in the sense that
there is no structural flaw that could lead to
incorrect assumptions and that the data does not
contain information that would lead to the incorrect
conclusion.
In order to use factor analysis to our advantage, it is
good to know what kind of factors beforehand we
expect. We expect that the factors that will be
evident are the their different types of knowledge.
There are four types of knowledge that we are
concerned with in this paper, which are taken from
NAEP 2009 science framework development: Issues
and recommendations [1]. The first is declarative
knowledge that deals with the "knowing that/what".
This is the knowledge that the students have
concerning basic scientific facts, concepts and
principles. The second form of knowledge is
Procedural knowledge. This corresponds to the
"knowing how", which is the students ability to
apply the facts, concepts and principles that they
know as well as knowing how to conduct simple
and complex procedures. The third is schematic
knowledge, which is the student’s explanations and
predictions about natural phenomena. This is also
known as the knowledge of "knowing why". The
last form that we will concern ourselves with is
strategic knowledge or"knowing when and where to
apply knowledge". This is how the students
transfers their current knowledge to new tasks and
problems.
